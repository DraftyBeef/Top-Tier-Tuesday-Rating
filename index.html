One rating, three engines

Every driver gets a single headline number, the TTR (Top Tier Rating), blended from three independent rating systems that each answer a different question. None of them use iRacing's iRating, Safety Rating, Strength of Field, or official points. The rating is built entirely from what happened on track in this league.

Wins and championships count extra

On top of the blend, every race win adds to a driver's TTR, and a season championship adds a further bonus and a golden trophy beside the driver's name. Championships are marked by league admins on the Roster tab.

Pace you can't fake

The Index's pace score is measured on sustained green-flag running, with out-laps and fresh-tire flyers stripped out, so a driver who looks fast only because they pitted for fresh tires more often doesn't get credit for speed they didn't really have. And because the two result-based engines carry most of the weight, raw pace can nudge a rating but never inflate it past where a driver actually finished.

Racecraft is about respect

The Index's racecraft score rewards racing cleanly around other cars. It's driven by contact with other cars, towed or wrecked laps, and on-track penalties (including pit-road), measured per lap, while solo mistakes like a lone spin or off-track stay out of it (those land in the Clean score instead). So a driver who races hard but gives room and rarely tangles with others rates highly, even if they make the occasional solo error.

Level-based and carried over

Ratings move both directions: a bad stretch costs you, a strong one gains you, measured against the field and your own established level. Ratings carry from season to season with no resets, but recent seasons carry meaningfully more weight than older ones, so a driver's number reflects how they're racing lately rather than treating a rusty old season the same as today. This reweights a driver's own seasons toward their recent form; a driver who only raced one season simply shows that season's level. Established drivers move slowly and stay stable; newer drivers swing more until they've built a record.

Provisional drivers

Anyone below the roster's race cutoff is marked Prov and can be hidden from the default standings, because a one-off result isn't a fair read on a driver. The cutoff is adjustable on the Roster tab, and any driver can be shown again with one click.
